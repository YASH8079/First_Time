PRD: Monitoring Improvements for Zone Navigator

SaaS Filter and Flow Health Tiles

📋 Product Requirements Document 👥 Monitoring Operations 🏷️ Zone Navigator
Priority: 1) SaaS filter in Flows  →  2) Flow health tiles
Audience: Product, Engineering, Design, and Monitoring Operations teams.

Executive Summary

Monitoring teams use two Zone Navigator views: All Customer Services and Flows. Both help but have key gaps.

  • All Customer Services offers SaaS filtering and tile-based duration views but lacks clear flow-level health, especially when flows span multiple Customer Services.
  • Flows shows detailed flow metrics and related Customer Services but lacks SaaS filtering and tile-based health history.

The proposed solution adds:

  1. Multi-select SaaS filter in Zone Navigator Flows
  2. Flow health tiles in Zone Navigator Flows

Together, these let monitors filter Flows by SaaS and see flow health duration without drilling into Customer Services.

Current Monitoring Workflow

Monitoring focuses on two Zone Navigator sections: All Customer Services and Flows. Monitors first check the last 30 minutes of health via 6 tiles (5 minutes each). Scope and severity rules apply after issue detection.

How All Customer Services Is Used Today

  • Monitors filter to SaaS products they own or watch.
  • They filter further to unhealthy Customer Services.
  • The 6 tiles show how long a Customer Service has been unhealthy.
  • If tiles cross thresholds, they open the Customer Service to find failing flows.

What works well: SaaS filtering reduces noise; tile patterns help spot sustained unhealthy conditions.

How Flows Is Used Today

  • Monitors use Flows to see unhealthy conditions at flow level.
  • Flows shows key metrics: availability, latency, success rate.
  • It shows which Customer Services use each flow and which are impacted.
  • Useful when one flow causes multiple Customer Services to be unhealthy.

What works well: Flows helps identify if one flow causes several unhealthy Customer Services.

Problem Statement

Monitors must switch views because no single view supports the full monitoring workflow efficiently.

Problem 1: Flows Has No SaaS Filter

  • Manual and slow when zones have hundreds of Customer Services.
  • Saved filters risk incompleteness as Customer Services change under a SaaS.
  • Risk of missing flows that need monitoring.
  • Monitors focus on SaaS ownership, not just fixed Customer Service lists.

This is the top priority gap.

Problem 2: Flows Has No Health Tile Visualization

  • Can't instantly see if a flow has been unhealthy for 5, 10, 15, or 30 minutes.
  • Must dig deeper to estimate duration.
  • This slows decisions during active monitoring.

Problem 3: Customer Service Tiles Hide Per-Flow Timing

Customer Service tiles aggregate multiple flows, masking flow-level differences and slowing decisions.

Example:

  • Flow A in Customer Service ABC failed success rate for 30 minutes.
  • Flow B in same service unhealthy on availability for 15 minutes.
  • Customer Service tiles may show all 6 tiles red.

Monitors can't tell which flow caused the issue first, which has been escalated, or which just crossed a threshold.

Problem 4: Shared Flows Create Repeated Manual Checks

  • From All Customer Services view, monitors can't tell if issues share the same root cause flow.
  • Monitors must open multiple Customer Services to confirm repeatedly.
  • This adds effort and delays diagnosis.

Goals and Non-Goals

Goals

Enable monitors to filter Flows by one or more SaaS products.
Ensure Flows auto-updates with Customer Service changes under SaaS.
Provide tile-based health history for flows matching Customer Service mental model.
Reduce repeated drill-downs for shared-flow issues.
Speed up and boost confidence in manual monitoring.

Non-Goals

No new alerting or notification behavior.
No changes to escalation policy logic.
No new save-filter features; current behavior is sufficient.

Proposed Requirements

Highest Priority

Feature 1: Multi-Select SaaS Filter in Zone Navigator Flows

Add a SaaS filter on the Flows page so monitors can select multiple SaaS products and view flows linked to Customer Services under those SaaS.

User Value

  • Avoids manually selecting many Customer Services.
  • Matches filtering to actual monitoring ownership.
  • Automatically includes new Customer Services under chosen SaaS.
  • Lowers risk of monitoring gaps.

Functional Requirements

  • Flows page must filter by one or more SaaS products.
  • Filter must work with existing filters like unhealthy status and others.
  • Selecting a SaaS shows flows of Customer Services mapped to it.
  • Changes in Customer Services under a SaaS update filter results automatically.
  • Feature must support existing saved-filter capability.

Expected Outcome

Monitors select SaaS products and instantly see relevant flows without managing static Customer Service lists.

Secondary Priority

Feature 2: Flow Health Tiles in Zone Navigator Flows

Add the same 6-tile health pattern from Customer Services to each flow in the Flows view.

User Value

  • Shows unhealthy duration per flow instantly.
  • Aligns monitoring views between Customer Services and Flows.
  • Helps spot long-term vs new issues.
  • Reduces opening multiple Customer Services to check timing.

Functional Requirements

  • Each flow shows 6 health tiles for the last 30 minutes.
  • Each tile covers a 5-minute interval.
  • MVP uses a single combined tile series per flow.
  • Tile color reflects SLO breach status across metrics.

Jobs to Be Done

When I open Flows for active monitoring, I want to filter by SaaS instead of manually curating Customer Services, so that I only see flows relevant to my scope and do not miss newly added Customer Services.
When I see a flow issue, I want to understand how long it has been unhealthy from the Flows page itself, so that I can act faster without repeated drill-downs.
When multiple Customer Services become unhealthy together, I want to see whether one shared flow is the common cause, so that I can diagnose once instead of checking each Customer Service individually.

User Stories

Feature 1: Multi-Select SaaS Filter

As a monitoring operator, I want to filter Flows by one or more SaaS products, so I see only flows relevant to my scope without manually selecting Customer Services.
As a monitoring operator, I want the SaaS filter to auto-include Customer Services mapped under selected SaaS, so new Customer Services aren't missed.
As a monitoring operator, I want the SaaS filter to work with existing filters like health status and saved filters, so I can maintain my workflow with less manual effort.
As a monitoring lead, I want monitors to filter by ownership scope instead of static CS lists, so operational coverage stays accurate when CS mappings change.

Feature 2: Flow Health Tiles

As a monitoring operator, I want each flow to show 6 health tiles for the last 30 minutes, so I quickly know how long a flow has been unhealthy.
As a monitoring operator, I want flow tiles to use 5-minute intervals like Customer Service tiles, so I can apply the same monitoring model across views.
As a monitoring operator, I want the combined flow tile to show the worst SLO state across availability, latency, and success rate, so I can spot unhealthy flows without opening each Customer Service.
As a monitoring operator, I want metric-wise tiles for availability, latency, and success rate if possible, so I can see which metric causes unhealthiness.

Key Scenarios

Scenario 1: Prevent Missing Newly Added Customer Services

A monitor saves a Flows view for coverage. Later, a new Customer Service is added under an existing SaaS. With Customer Service filtering, it might be missed. With SaaS filtering, it appears automatically.

Scenario 2: Shared Flow Impacts Many Customer Services

A flow reused in several Customer Services becomes unhealthy. Many appear unhealthy, but the monitor can see the common flow once and identify all impacted Services immediately.

Scenario 3: Different Flows Fail at Different Times

One flow fails for 30 minutes, another starts failing after 15 minutes. Customer Service tiles can't show this clearly. Flow tiles show exact timing per flow.

Scenario 4: Success Rate Issue Hidden in Aggregation

A flow fails on success rate while the Customer Service shows aggregated unhealthiness. Flow-level view reveals exact metric and timing without relying on summary health.

Design and UX Considerations

  • The SaaS filter should behave consistently with existing filter patterns in Zone Navigator.
  • Multi-select behavior is required.
  • Saved filters should continue to work with the new SaaS filter dimension.
  • Empty results can continue using current blank-state behavior.
  • Flow health tiles should be visually consistent with existing Customer Service health tiles to reuse the current monitoring mental model.

Dependencies and Assumptions

  • The system already knows the mapping between SaaS and Customer Services.
  • The system already computes the health signals needed for flow metrics (availability, latency, success rate).
  • Saved filter capability already exists and does not need redesign.

Risks and Open Considerations

⚠️Combined tiles simplify the view, but may still hide which metric specifically caused the unhealthy state.
⚠️If engineering effort is manageable, metric-wise tiles may deliver better operational value than combined tiles alone.
⚠️Performance should be reviewed if SaaS filtering significantly expands or changes how flow result sets are computed.

Success Metrics

  • Reduction in time spent manually selecting or maintaining Customer Service filters in Flows.
  • Reduction in repeated drill-downs across multiple Customer Services for shared-flow incidents.
  • Improved operator confidence that all in-scope Customer Services under selected SaaS are covered.
  • Faster time to understand whether a flow issue is newly emerging or long-running.
